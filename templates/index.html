What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack where attackers attempt to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, banking details, or personal data by pretending to be a trusted organization.

Identify a Phishing Email

Look at the email below and identify the warning signs.

From: support-paypal@gmail.com

To: john@example.com

Subject: Urgent! Verify Your Account Immediately

Dear Customer,

We noticed unusual activity on your account. Your account will be permanently suspended unless you verify it within the next 24 hours.

Click here:
http://paypal-secure-login.xyz

Thank you.

Warning Signs

  • ❌ Sender uses a free Gmail address instead of an official company domain.
  • ❌ Creates urgency to pressure you into acting quickly.
  • ❌ Generic greeting ("Dear Customer") instead of your name.
  • ❌ Suspicious link that does not match the official website.
  • ❌ Threatens account suspension to create fear.

Best Practices

  • ✔ Verify the sender's email address.
  • ✔ Never click suspicious links.
  • ✔ Check website URLs carefully.
  • ✔ Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
  • ✔ Keep your software updated.
  • ✔ Report suspicious emails to your organization.
